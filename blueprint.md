INZU: The Laws of Minimalist Authority

> "Design is not just what it looks like and feels like. Design is how it works." — Steve Jobs

This document captures the **frontend UX and visual design principles** for Inzu. It is the companion to the core product and backend blueprint described in `blueprint.md`.

## 1. The Law of the "Single Truth"

High-Net-Worth individuals pay for clarity, not data.

- **Rule**: Every screen must have one primary focal point. If the user has to look for the "important part," we have failed.
- **Application**: On the home screen, the focal point is the Net Collection. Not the number of tenants, not the number of tickets—just the money. Everything else is a "sub-layer."

## 2. The Forest Green Anchor

We do not use green for "Success" or "Money." We use it for Structure.

- **Rule**: Use the Deep Forest Green (`#2D4B3E`) as the "chassis" of the app. It should feel like the heavy door of a Mercedes G-Wagon.
- **Composition**: Use the green as a solid, unmoving block. Pair it with extreme amounts of white space (`Silver Silk` `#F5F7F6`) to let the typography breathe.

## 3. The "Invisible" UI (Ive’s Doctrine)

The best interface is the one you don't notice.

- **Rule**: No unnecessary borders, no drop shadows, no "glare."
- **Correction**: If a line doesn't serve a functional purpose to separate data, delete it. Use alignment and space to create groups, not boxes.
- **Materiality**: Elements should feel like Glass, Titanium, and Leather. Use Background Blur (Frosted Glass) for overlays to maintain a sense of depth and luxury.

## 4. Typography as Engineering

In Inzu, text is a structural element.

- **Rule**: Never use more than two font weights.
- **Style**: Use Semi-Bold for the "Truth" (the numbers) and Regular for the context.
- **Letter Spacing**: Tighten the tracking (-0.02em) on large numbers to make them feel "heavy" and authoritative.

## 5. The "Haptic" Motion

Since we are targeting "Premium," the app must move like a luxury watch.

- **Rule**: No "snapping."
- **Motion**: Every transition must have a Bezier curve. It should start slow, speed up, and end with a soft, dampened stop. It should feel like a hydraulic drawer closing.

## 6. Zero-Friction AI (Frontend Behavior)

AI should not be a "Chatbot" that says "Hello." It should be an invisible assistant.

- **Rule**: AI doesn't ask; it proposes.
- **Application (UI Copy & Flows)**: Instead of a notification saying "You have a new ticket," the UI presents:  
  "Tenant in B4 requested a plumber. I have scheduled 'Alpha Plumbers' for 2 PM. Confirm?"  
  One button. Zero effort.

## 7. The Pininfarina Accent (The Red Line)

- **Rule**: We only use our accent red (`#E22026`) for things that require the landlord’s literal heart rate to increase.
- **Application**: Arrears exceeding 30 days or emergency structural damage. If everything is red, nothing is red.

## 8. The Final Litmus Test (The "Jobs" Question)

Before shipping any **frontend feature or visual change**, ask:

1. Can we take one more thing away?
2. Does this feel like it was built by a carpenter or a coder? (We want the carpenter.)
3. Would this look good etched into a piece of aluminum?
